Ana Bikhayr
Basha habitually creates paper boats, these paper boats are, however, after putting them on the water, she makes them sink, like her self-esteem and self-worth. She is constantly picked on by her schoolmates, water is splashed on her, insulting looks are thrown at her, and fingers are mockingly pointed towards her. But that doesn’t stop there, she also receives physical and verbal bullying from her classmates. All because of one mediocre reason, she is a Muslim. Even though Basha has done nothing wrong to them, they just won’t leave her alone, in person, and in her thoughts. Despite this, fortune still shines on her and she is given a chance to prove herself.

Decalcomania
A filmmaker dies twice- once when they stop filming, and this first death is the most painful. The moment you lose the most meaningful thing of life,’ you are no longer alive, only existence. Decalcomania is an experimental film that is going to take us on an emotional journey. The character delivered the confession as a filmmaker who has truly learned what film means to her in an in-depth way through the autobiographical poetic-experimental film. Filmmakers face their shadows as a filmmaker. They fear how they might one day be numb to the emotional power of film. This highlights issues of passion or work becoming a burden and trying to overcome it to be joyful again.

Farasha
As a young Muslimah high school achiever, Farasha never had it easy pursuing her passion for filmmaking. Her parents didn't like the fact that the one thing she loves is something far more challenging to accept in their religion. As her parents started disagreeing with her passion, she felt more trapped, as if her wings were being held, like she was being confined in a cage. Farasha is standing between a thin line on whether to keep going or stop since she doesn't have the right to live as per her choice. Farasha developed a disorder involving behavioral challenges that could harm her self-image. While battling her condition, Farasha tries to find her freedom to flight or fight in a situation where it was never an option.

Sa Layag ng Bangkang Paurong
Alaminos City is known as the “Home of the Hundred Islands,” which tourists dominate. This is because of the scarce wealth, culture, and scenery found in over one hundred islands, and only the bodies of water serve as divisions. Because of the rich marine life that constitutes it. Milboy, an Alaminos City National High School student, has been involved in boating and transporting tourists for seven years to help with their family’s expenses and support himself in terms of his education. Many people like him come into this job at a very young age that caused them to leave school or get a low grade, but can this backward voyage prevent their future voyage? Will Milboy still sailing toward his dreams? Witness the journey of life of a young boatman, Milboy.

Ang Himala sa Kamay ng Daang Tao
From an island of love to that of faith and devotion, the people of Alaminos City, Pangasinan, have transformed one of the provinces’ world-famous Hundred Islands into a pilgrimage site for the faithful. The 56-foot statue of Christ the Savior was miraculously built through the Bayanihan system of Alaminians, considering its impracticable way of constructing. The collaboration between the church and state made it eco-faith tourism, increased tourist arrivals and the City’s progression.
